Quantum Computing

The Basics

What is Quantum Computing?

Quantum computers are new kinds of machines that promise an exponential growth spurt in processing power. It will also have the ability to solve problems regular computers cannot. This is possible because the computer is based upon properties of Quantum Mechanics like Superposition and Entanglement. Regular computers use a Bit to store data in 0s and 1s on the other hand Quantum computers use Qubits which can be a combination of a 0 and or 1.

Superpostion and Entanglement

Superposition is a quantum state where the Qubit can be a 0 or 1 at any given moment. Entanglement is a version of Superposition which is really interesting. It allows to have two Qubits in a Superposition state that is basically combined as 1 Qubit but can be read as a 0 or a 1.

Purpose and Usefullness

It is currently impossible for our laptops to calculate what a molecule would do to our body like drugs and this is a good example why the pharmaceutical industry spends so much money on testing. But with Quantum computing experts say that all of the testing could be done not on live test subjects but in a database. Because Quantum computers can analyze large quantities of data and spot patterns quickly, they could also tackle optimization problems for transportation and industry, advanced modeling, and boost artificial intelligence.

Drawback #1: Cooling

The big downside with building a computer of this complexity is cooling it. In order to make use of the diffrent Quantum states like Superpostion and Entanglement the CPU that controls the whole thing has to be 10-15 millikelvin (-270c). This takes an entire infastructure of refrigeration that relies on dilution refrigerators. As well as there’s an entire suite of hardware components like coaxial cables, attenuators, microwave amplifiers, circulators, a whole bevy of components that all need to function at low temperatures.

Drawback #2: Errors

To control the Qubits there is a resonator that communicates to the Qubit by sending pulses. The biggest challenge is that Superpostion and Entanglement are very fragile and if anything goes wrong it will cause the entire network of information to collapse. In regular computers people have overcome this by writing error correcting algorithms that compare copies of the output then they select the most regular answer and remove the rest of the data. But companies like IBM and Google are still figuring out how to correct errors on Quantum computers. Unexpectedly this brings more complexity to the computer's architecture and therefore its prone to more errors.

It's Future

I think that companies should come together and make a universal architecture in order for quantum computers to run smoothly and advance at a good pace. If this gets improved on I think the industry will get the momentum it needs in order to be a viable thing that maybe more companies will start investing in. With all of that said its still just going to be just a big piece of tech that only companies will own and sell services to consumers like server databases because it wont be portable or affordable as of now.
